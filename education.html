What is Crypto?

Think of crypto as a new kind of money that's growing up. It's kind of like learning how to ride a bike - it might seem hard at first, but someday it could be a whole new way of doing things!

Here are some things to remember about crypto:

It's digital money that exists on computers.

It's not controlled by banks.

It's secure because of cryptography.

It's not yet widely used for buying things, but it might be in the future.

The History of Cryptocurrency

Bitcoin arrives (2009): A mysterious person (or group) called Satoshi Nakamoto created Bitcoin, the first ever cryptocurrency. It was like a whole new way of using digital money, with its own special code and secure system The blockchain revolution: Bitcoin relied on something called blockchain, a fancy way of saying a shared record book that everyone can see but no one can change. This made crypto super secure and transparent. Crypto goes wild (2010s): More and more cryptocurrencies started popping up, each with its own twist on the original idea. The value of some cryptocurrencies skyrocketed, then crashed, then went up again - it was a wild ride!
